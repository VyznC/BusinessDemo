FOOTBALL

Association football, more commonly known as simply football or soccer, is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players who primarily use their feet to propel the ball around a rectangular field called a pitch

Equipment: Football (or soccer ball) Shinpads

Olympic: Men's since the 1900 Olympics and women's since the 1996 Olympics

Venue: Football pitch (also known as football field, football ground, soccer field, soccer pitch or simply "pitch")

First played: Mid-19th century England

Highest governing body: FIFA

Mixed-sex: No, separate competitions

Team members: 11 per side (including goalkeeper)

AMERICAN FOOTBALL

American football, also referred to simply as football in the United States and Canada, and also known as gridiron, is a team sport played by two teams of eleven players on a rectangular field with goalposts at each end.

Olympic: No (demonstrated at the 1932 Summer Olympics)

Equipment: Football; Football helmet; Pads (shoulder, thigh, knee, and optionally elbow pads)

Contact: Full

World Games: Yes (invitational sport at 2005, 2017, and 2022 (flag football) Games)

Country or region: Worldwide (most popular in North America)

First played: November 6, 1869; New Brunswick, New Jersey, United States; (Princeton vs. Rutgers)

Highest governing body: International Federation of American Football
